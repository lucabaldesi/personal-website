--- name: Colosseum website: https://colosseum.net image: https://www.northeastern.edu/colosseum/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/colosseum_logo7_512-1.png code_url: https://github.com/lucabaldesi/openwifi/tree/ack_reception_interval chocolate: 98 --- Colosseum is a wireless network channel emulator with hardware in the loop[[1]](javascript:toggle_display('colosseum-colosseum')) sporting 256 USRP Software Defined Radios designed and released by DARPA in 2019. * Despite being a flexible network emulator, standard SDR cannot support 802.11 packet timing requirements; designed, implemented, and deployed an extension using Zylinx System-on-chip boards and [OpenWiFi](https://github.com/open-sdr/openwifi) to fully enable Wi-Fi communications [[2]](javascript:toggle_display('colosseum-Baldesi2022Charm')). 
A. Chaudhari and M. Braun, “A Scalable FPGA Architecture for Flexible, Large-Scale, Real-Time RF Channel Emulation,” in 2018 13th International Symposium on Reconfigurable Communication-centric Systems-on-Chip (ReCoSoC), 2018, pp. 1–8. doi: 10.1109/ReCoSoC.2018.8449390.
